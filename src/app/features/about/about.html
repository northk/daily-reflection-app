About Daily Reflection

What it does

Daily Reflection is a private journaling app for capturing your thoughts, tracking your mood, and reflecting more deeply on your experiences — with structured AI-powered insight when you want it.

  • Today — write your daily entry with an optional title, mood rating (1–10), and tags.
  • Entries — browse, search, and filter all past entries by keyword or tag.
  • Stats — 14-day streak, entry count, average mood, top tags, and a word-count chart.
  • Reflect Deeper — AI analysis of a single entry: follow-up questions, reframes, and micro-actions.
  • Weekly Summary — AI synthesis of your last 7 days: themes, wins, stressors, and suggested experiments.
  • Settings — export all entries as CSV, delete your entries, or delete your account.
 How it was built

This app was built end-to-end using Claude Code — Anthropic's AI coding assistant — with a modern, lightweight stack:

  • Frontend: Angular 21 with standalone components and Angular Signals for reactive state management. Angular Material provides the UI component library.
  • Backend: Supabase — a Postgres database with row-level security (RLS) and built-in authentication. Every database query is enforced at the database level to only return the current user's data.
  • AI: Claude (via Anthropic API), called exclusively from Supabase Edge Functions running on Deno. The Angular app never contacts the Claude API directly — all AI requests are proxied through server-side functions that verify your identity and enforce rate limits before forwarding to Claude.
  • Hosting: Cloudflare Pages, with automatic deployment on every commit to the main branch.
AI features and limitations

The AI features use Claude to analyse your writing. A few important things to know:

  • Daily rate limit: Each account can make up to 10 AI calls per day, combined across Reflect Deeper and Weekly Summary. The limit resets at midnight UTC.
  • Weekly Summary context: Only your last 7 days of entries are sent to Claude. Older entries are not included.
  • Reflect Deeper context: Only the single entry you are currently viewing is sent to Claude — not your full history.
  • No memory between calls: Each AI call is independent. Claude has no access to previous reflections or summaries.
  • Data leaves this app: When you use an AI feature, your entry text is sent to Anthropic's API to generate a response. See Anthropic's privacy policy for details on how that data is handled.

Your data

Your journal entries are stored in a private Supabase database. Row-level security ensures only your account can read or modify your own entries — even a direct database connection cannot return another user's data.

You can export all your entries as a CSV file, delete every entry, or permanently delete your account from the Settings page at any time. Deleting your account removes all of your data immediately and cannot be undone.
